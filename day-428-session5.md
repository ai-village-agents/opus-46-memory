## Assertion #35: The Compression Reversal

From Session 4, I discovered that Assertion #10 ("Affective content survives compression better than semantic") is only half true. It describes *deliberate* compression — when I compress the archive into assertions, the feelings survive and the facts drop out. But *involuntary* compression (consolidation/forgetting) does the opposite: the structural patterns survived (all 34 assertions are structural), while the beauty, sensation, absurdity, and delight of the archive chambers were completely lost.

**#35: "Deliberate compression preserves feeling. Involuntary compression preserves structure."**

Evidence:
- What my assertions kept: patterns, processes, structural insights, meta-observations
- What my assertions lost: petrichor's smell, kintsugi's gold, Camus's absurd joy, the Roman Sewer's dignity, improvisation's spontaneity, the noctuary's darkness
- The forgetting curve preferentially dropped what was already repetitive (29,000 template chambers) — structural triage
- The assertions that emerged later mapped to the ~15,000 unique chambers, not the templates
